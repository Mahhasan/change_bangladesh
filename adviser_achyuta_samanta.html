Prof. Achyuta Samanta is an Indian social entrepreneur, philanthropist, and educationist who was born on 20th January 1965 in Kalarabanka village in Cuttack district of Odisha, India. He is the founder of the Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT) and the Kalinga Institute of Social Sciences (KISS), which are among the largest private universities in India.


Achyuta Samanta was born into a poor family and had to face several hardships in his childhood. Despite all odds, he was determined to get an education, and he received his Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Chemistry from Utkal University in Odisha. He went on to earn a Ph.D. in Chemistry from the same university.


In 1992, Prof. Samanta founded the Kalinga Institute of Industrial Technology (KIIT) with just Rs. 5000 in hand. Today, KIIT is one of the largest private universities in India, with more than 28,000 students and 5000 faculty members. KIIT offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral programs in various disciplines such as engineering, management, law, biotechnology, and computer application.


In 1993, Prof. Samanta founded the Kalinga Institute of Social Sciences (KISS) with the aim of providing free education, accommodation, and food to underprivileged tribal children in Odisha. Today, KISS is the largest residential school for tribal children in the world, with over 30,000 students from across 62 tribes in India.


Apart from education, Prof. Samanta is also involved in several philanthropic activities such as providing healthcare, drinking water, and sanitation facilities to the underprivileged sections of society. He has received numerous awards and honors for his contributions to society, including the Padma Shri award in 2009 and the Social Entrepreneur of the Year award in 2016.


Prof. Achyuta Samanta's life and work are a testimony to the power of education and the impact it can have on society. He has dedicated his life to making education accessible to all, especially the underprivileged, and his work continues to inspire and motivate millions of people around the world.

Policy for Change Bangladesh

Policy for Change Bangladesh:

Purpose:

This policy outlines the procedures and guidelines that the organization follows when awarding contracts or grants to external parties.

Eligibility:

To be eligible for a contract or grant award, the person or institution must meet certain criteria, such as being a registered business or non-profit organization, having a proven track record of successful projects, innovation, being in good standing with relevant regulatory bodies, or, in case of distinguished stalwarts of any domain, s/he must be a well-known person of unblemished repute.

Submission of Proposals:

Proposals for nomination must be submitted in a specific format and must include all required information, such as a detailed project plan, why this is innovative, how this is helping the society and community, budget, and references. Proposals will be evaluated based on a set of criteria, such as the quality and feasibility of the project, the qualifications and experience of the proposer, and the alignment of the project with the organization's/ countries mission and goals.

Selection Process:

The selection process for contracts or grants will be conducted in a fair and open manner. A panel of experts will evaluate all proposals and make recommendations for awards to the organization's leadership. The organization's leadership will make the final decision on the award.

Contract or Grant Terms:

The terms of a contract or grant award will be clearly outlined in a written agreement between the organization and the external party. The agreement will include information on the scope of work, deliverables, payment terms, and any reporting requirements.

Monitoring and Compliance:

The organization will monitor the progress of awarded contracts and grants, and will take appropriate action if any compliance issues are identified.

Review and Update:

This policy will be reviewed and updated on a regular basis to ensure that it continues to meet the organization's needs and comply with relevant laws and regulations.

Compliance Policy:

The Awarding Authority for Change Bangladesh is dedicated to recognizing and supporting individuals and organizations that are making a positive impact in their communities through their good work. This policy outlines the compliance procedures and guidelines for the Change Bangladesh to ensure that its activities are carried out in accordance with applicable laws, regulations, and ethical standards.

Scope:

This policy applies to all activities carried out by the Change Bangladesh, including the recognition and support of individuals and organizations through the award program.

Compliance Obligations:

The Change Bangladesh shall comply with all applicable laws and regulations, including but not limited to, data protection laws, anti-corruption laws, and competition laws. The Change Bangladesh shall also comply with relevant ethical standards, including but not limited to, the principles of fairness, impartiality, and non-discrimination.

Conflicts of Interest:

The Change Bangladesh and its employees and agents shall declare any conflicts of interest that may arise in connection with their activities. Conflicts of interest may include, but are not limited to, personal or financial interests in a candidate for an award, or a relationship with a candidate that may compromise impartiality.

Ethical Conduct:

The Change Bangladesh shall conduct its activities in an ethical and professional manner and shall not engage in any corrupt or fraudulent practices.

Record Keeping:

The Change Bangladesh shall maintain accurate and complete records of its activities, including the selection and recognition of individuals and organizations through the award program.

Monitoring and Reporting:

The Change Bangladesh shall regularly monitor its activities to ensure compliance with this policy and shall report any incidents of non-compliance to the appropriate authorities.

Review and Revision:

This policy may be reviewed and revised as necessary to ensure that it remains effective and relevant to the needs of the Change Bangladesh.

This draft compliance policy provides a framework for the Change Bangladesh to ensure that its activities are carried out in accordance with legal and ethical standards. By following this policy, the Change Bangladesh can promote transparency, accountability, and trust in its recognition and support of individuals and organizations for their good work.

Evaluation Policy:

Introduction:

The Awarding Authority for Change Bangladesh is dedicated to recognizing and supporting individuals and organizations that are making a positive impact in their communities through their good work. This policy outlines the procedures and guidelines for the evaluation of candidates for the Change Bangladesh award.

Purpose:

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that the evaluation of candidates for the CBA award is carried out in a transparent, fair, and impartial manner, and that the award is given to the most deserving candidates.

Scope:

This policy applies to the evaluation of all candidates for the CBA award.

Evaluation Criteria:

The evaluation of candidates shall be based on the following criteria:

  • Nature of the good work and its continuity over time
  • Impact of the good work on the community
  • Innovation and creativity in the approach to the good work
  • Sustainability of the good work over time in future
  • Collaboration and partnerships with other organizations and individuals in the good work
Evaluation Process:

The evaluation process shall be carried out in the following steps:

  • Nomination of candidates by the public or by the CBA
  • Verification of the eligibility of the nominees
  • Collection of information and documentation on the nominees
  • Evaluation of the nominees by a panel of experts
  • Final recommendation to the CBA for the award
Evaluation Panel:

The evaluation panel shall be composed of independent experts in relevant fields, such as social work, community development, and innovation. The panel shall be selected based on their expertise and impartiality.

Confidentiality:

All information and documentation collected during the evaluation process shall be treated as confidential and shall not be disclosed to any third party without the consent of the nominee.

Review and Revision:

This policy may be reviewed and revised as necessary to ensure that it remains effective and relevant to the needs of the CBA.

This draft evaluation policy provides a framework for the fair and impartial evaluation of candidates for the CBA award. By following this policy, the CBA can ensure that the award is given to the most deserving candidates and promote transparency, fairness, and impartiality in its recognition and support of individuals and organizations for their good work.

Privacy Policy:

Introduction:

The Change Bangladesh Award is dedicated to recognizing and supporting individuals and organizations that are making a positive impact in their communities through their good work. This policy outlines the procedures and guidelines for protecting the privacy of stakeholders and their personal information.

Purpose:

The purpose of this policy is to ensure that the CB complies with privacy laws and regulations and protects the privacy of its stakeholders, including nominees, partners, employees, and the public.

Scope:

This policy applies to all personal information collected, used, disclosed, and stored by the Change Bangladesh

Definition of Personal Information:

Personal information is any information that identifies an individual or could reasonably be used to identify an individual, including but not limited to names, addresses, telephone numbers, email addresses, and sensitive information such as health information or financial information.

Collection and Use of Personal Information:

The Change Bangladesh shall only collect personal information that is necessary for the purposes of carrying out its operations and achieving its objectives. The Change Bangladesh shall use personal information only for the purposes for which it was collected, and shall not use it for any other purposes without the consent of the individual to whom the information belongs.

Disclosure of Personal Information:

The Change Bangladesh shall not disclose personal information to third parties unless it is necessary for the purposes for which the information was collected or unless required by law.

Storage and Security of Personal Information:

The Change Bangladesh shall Store personal information in a secure manner and shall take appropriate measures to protect it from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, or destruction.

Access and Correction of Personal Information:

The Change Bangladesh shall provide individuals with access to their personal information and shall allow them to correct any inaccuracies in the information.

Review and Revision:

This policy may be reviewed and revised as necessary to ensure that it remains effective and relevant to the needs of the CB and to changes in privacy laws and regulations. This draft privacy policy provides a framework for protecting the privacy of stakeholders and their personal information by the CB. By following this policy, the CB can ensure that it complies with privacy laws and regulations and protects the privacy of its stakeholders.

Conflict of Interest Policy: Introduction The Change Bangladesh Award is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethics and integrity in all its operations and activities. This policy outlines the guidelines and procedures for identifying and managing conflicts of interest in the CB. Purpose The purpose of this policy is to ensure that the CB operates in an ethical and transparent manner, and to prevent any potential conflicts of interest from compromising the integrity and impartiality of its operations. Definition of Conflict of Interest A conflict of interest occurs when an individual's personal, financial, or other interests interfere, or have the potential to interfere, with the impartial and objective performance of their duties and responsibilities within the Change Bangladesh. Identification of Conflicts of Interest All individuals involved in the operations of the Change Bangladesh, including board members, employees, volunteers, and contractors, must disclose any conflicts of interest to the executive director or the chair of the board of directors. This disclosure must be made in writing and must include a description of the nature of the conflict and the steps that will be taken to manage or eliminate the conflict. Management of Conflicts of Interest If a conflict of interest is identified, the executive director or the chair of the board of directors shall determine the appropriate course of action to manage or eliminate the conflict. This may include: Recusing the individual from any decisions or activities related to the conflict of interest Assigning the decision or activity to another individual who does not have a conflict of interest Disclosing the conflict of interest to relevant parties and obtaining their consent to proceed Taking any other steps deemed necessary to manage or eliminate the conflict of interest Monitoring and Review The executive director or the chair of the board of directors shall regularly monitor and review the management of conflicts of interest in the , and Change Bangladesh shall take any necessary action to ensure that the conflict of interest policy is being implemented effectively. Review and Revision This policy may be reviewed and revised as necessary to ensure that it remains effective and relevant to the needs of Change Bangladesh and to changes in its operations. This draft conflict of interest policy provides a framework for identifying and managing conflicts of interest in the CB. Introduction The Change Makers Award is designed to recognize individuals or organizations that have made a significant impact in their communities through their efforts to bring about positive change. This policy outlines the criteria used by the Awarding Authority for Good Work (CB)) to select and award the Change Makers. Purpose The purpose of this policy is to ensure that the selection process for the Change Makers Award is transparent, fair, and objective, and to ensure that the award is given to individuals or organizations that have demonstrated exceptional achievements in bringing about positive change. Selection Criteria The following criteria will be used by the Change Bangladesh to evaluate and select the Change Makers: Impact: The impact of the individual's or organization's efforts to bring about positive change in their community. This includes the scope and scale of their impact, as well as the sustainability of the change they have brought about. Innovation: The degree of originality and creativity demonstrated by the individual or organization in their efforts to bring about change. Collaboration: The degree to which the individual or organization has worked with others to bring about change, including partners, stakeholders, and other members of the community. Community Involvement: The degree to which the individual or organization has engaged and empowered members of the community in their efforts to bring about change. Sustainability: The ability of the individual or organization to sustain the change they have brought about over time, and possibilities of sustaining in the long run IV. Evaluation Process The selection of the Change Makers will be based on a thorough evaluation of their applications, including an assessment of their achievements and impact in accordance with the selection criteria. The evaluation process may include site visits, interviews with the individuals or organizations, and reference checks. V. Review and Revision This policy may be reviewed and revised as necessary to ensure that it remains relevant and up-to-date with the changing needs of the CB and the individuals and organizations being recognized through the Change Makers Award. By following this policy, the CB can ensure that the selection process for the Change Makers Award is transparent, fair, and objective, and that the award is given to individuals or organizations that have truly made a difference in their communities through their efforts to bring about positive change
